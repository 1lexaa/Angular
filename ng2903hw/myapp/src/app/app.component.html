Bill Gates Biography

Early life

William Henry Gates III was born on October 28, 1955, in Seattle, Washington. Gates grew up in an upper-middle-class family with his older sister, Kristianne, and younger sister, Libby. Their father, William H. Gates Sr., was a prominent lawyer, and their mother, Mary Maxwell Gates, served on charitable boards and corporate boards, including those of First Interstate BancSystem and the United Way.

Career

Gates began programming computers at the age of 13. In 1973, he enrolled at Harvard University but dropped out two years later to start Microsoft with childhood friend Paul Allen. The company's big break came in 1980, when IBM approached Microsoft to create an operating system for its new personal computer. Microsoft retained the rights to the operating system, now known as MS-DOS, and became a key player in the emerging personal computer industry.

Philanthropy

Gates and his wife, Melinda, are known for their philanthropic work through the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation. The foundation focuses on global health and development, education, and public policy. In 2010, Gates and Warren Buffett launched The Giving Pledge, a campaign to encourage billionaires to give away the majority of their wealth to charitable causes.
